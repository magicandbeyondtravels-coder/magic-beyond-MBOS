# Customer Journey

The canonical ten-stage journey every Magic & Beyond Travels family moves through, from first sensing the trip decision ahead to referring others or returning for the next one. Every other domain in MBOS references these stage names; none may redefine them (ARCHITECTURE.md section 3.2, rule 4). The order follows README.md's "Customer journey philosophy": Discover → Learn → Trust → Plan → Book → Prepare → Travel → Reflect → Refer → Return.

The throughline across all ten stages is the transformation in [customer-transformation.md](../core/customer-transformation.md): a family moves from overwhelmed, unsure and afraid of the wrong decision, to confident, clear and focused on making memories. The business's job, per [positioning.md](../core/positioning.md), is not to simplify the trip — it is to simplify the decision.

| Stage | Customer mindset | Customer questions | Business role | Relevant domains |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Discover | Just starting to sense the scale of the decision ahead: destinations, dates, a multigenerational group to coordinate. | "Where should we even go?" "Is this the right kind of trip for our family?" | Be visible where overwhelmed families start looking, and signal early that a different kind of help exists. | marketing, content, core |
| Learn | Actively researching, comparing options, absorbing conflicting advice from every direction. | "What are our actual options?" "What's realistic for our budget, our kids' ages, our whole group?" | Educate without adding to the noise; organize the decision instead of supplying more information. | content, marketing, offerings |
| Trust | Narrowing down who deserves to help, and testing whether this advisor understands multigenerational family travel specifically. | "Does this person actually get families like mine?" "Why this business over researching it myself?" | Demonstrate the differentiators and make the brand promise concrete and specific. | core, marketing, customers |
| Plan | Committed to working with the business; anxiety easing as someone else helps hold the decision. | "What exactly should we book?" "How does this fit our whole family and our budget?" | Deliver the actual decision-simplifying planning process the mission promises. | offerings, operations, customers |
| Book | Ready to commit financially; wants confidence, not second-guessing. | "Am I making the right final call?" "What if something changes later?" | Make the commitment moment smooth and reinforce confidence rather than leave room for doubt. | offerings, sales, operations |
| Prepare | Anticipation building; practical logistics replace decision anxiety. | "What do we need to do or bring before we go?" "What should we expect day to day?" | Hand off from decision-support to logistics-support with clear, calm guidance. | operations, content, customers |
| Travel | Living the experience, ideally already in the "after" state: confident, excited, clear. | "Is everything going as planned?" "Who do I contact if something goes wrong?" | Support without hovering; be the safety net that protects the memory-making. | operations, customers, systems |
| Reflect | Processing the trip afterward, deciding whether it was worth it, including the planning itself. | "Was it worth it?" "Did the planning process actually make a difference?" | Invite reflection and capture the outcome; reinforce the memories half of the tagline. | marketing, customers, content |
| Refer | If the transformation held, the family becomes an advocate for others stuck where they were. | "Who else do I know who's overwhelmed the way I was?" "How do I introduce them to this?" | Make referral easy and tie it directly to the transformation story just lived. | marketing, sales, customers |
| Return | Considering the next trip with overwhelm already lowered, since trust is established. | "Where should we go next?" "Do we come back to the same advisor?" | Nurture the relationship for the next decision; re-enter the cycle at Plan, not Discover. | marketing, offerings, customers |
